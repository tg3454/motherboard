## 2. The Authority Matrix & Reserved Matters

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **No Implied Authority:** Titles (e.g., Fork Lead, Executive Officer), badges, email addresses, repository admin access, or historical actions do **not** confer authority to act on behalf of GOBITSNBYTES FOUNDATION. Authority flows only through written delegation or Board resolutions.

### 2.1 Reserved Matters (Board Only)
The following powers are permanently reserved for the Board of Directors and cannot be delegated:
* Amendment of e-MOA or e-AOA.
* Amendment of the Authority Matrix.
* Admission or termination of legal Members.
* Appointment or removal of Directors.
* Winding up, dissolution, or amalgamation of the Foundation.
* Transaction approvals creating a conflict of interest for a Director.

### 2.2 Decision Levels
* **Full Authority (✅):** Independent execution within defined thresholds.
* **Dual Auth Required (🔁):** Requires two named roles to approve.
* **Recommend / Escalate (📋):** Propose or initiate, but requires upstream sign-off.
* **No Authority (🚫):** Prohibited from executing or approving.

### 2.3 Legal & Contracts
* No Fork Lead, volunteer, or contributor may sign any agreement, Memorandum of Understanding (MoU), venue contract, or commercial commitment on behalf of the Foundation or a Fork.
* Authority to bind the Foundation exists strictly under Board resolution or express written delegation.

## 3. Financial Rules & Limits

> [!CAUTION]
> **Zero Tolerance for Informal Routing:** All funds connected with bits&bytes™ or any Fork must be accepted, recorded, and routed through Foundation-controlled accounts.

### 3.1 Hard Prohibitions
* **No Personal UPI Routing:** Absolutely no Paytm, GPay, PhonePe, or personal UPI collections for event fees, sponsorships, or donations.
* **No Cash Collections:** No cash handling or cash storage outside approved Foundation programs.
* **No Informal Bank Accounts:** No local or unofficial bank accounts may be opened in the name of a Fork or under the bits&bytes™ brand.
* **No Pass-through Accounts:** No routing of Foundation funds through third-party accounts or personal wallets.

### 3.2 Dual Authorization
* Payments exceeding thresholds established in the Authority Matrix require dual approval.
* **Self-Approval Prohibition:** No person may approve a payment to themselves. The CFO and CEO cannot approve their own expenses or payouts.

### 3.3 Sponsorships
* Forks are permitted to seek local sponsors (e.g., for food, venue, or swag).
* All sponsorship agreements must be legally executed upstream. Sponsorship money must flow directly to the Foundation's bank accounts. The Foundation then allocates and dispatches these funds back to the Fork's approved budget.
* The Financial Year runs from **1 April to 31 March**.

## 4. Fork Governance & Onboarding

Forks are operational units of GOBITSNBYTES FOUNDATION, not separate legal entities or franchises. They must operate within the Foundation's standards.

### 4.1 Onboarding Journey
New Forks must complete the following 7 steps in sequence:
1. **Accept GitHub Invite:** Access organization templates and codebase repositories.
2. **Join Leads Discord:** Access the private coordination server.
3. **Setup Email:** Configure `[city]@gobitsnbytes.org` (e.g., `delhi@gobitsnbytes.org`).
4. **Deploy Website:** Set up the local landing page.
5. **Share Notion:** Link local workspace databases with Upstream.
6. **Submit First Pulse:** Run the `/pulse` command to establish activity tracking.
7. **Define Team & Plan Event:** Register roles and outline the first event.

### 4.2 Fork Rules & Lifecycle
* **Teen-Led Requirement:** Fork Leads must be active students/teenagers. If a Fork loses its teen leadership, it loses its Fork status. Adults are prohibited from running Forks.
* **Stale Fork Detection:**
  * The provisioning engine checks pulse activity weekly.
  * If a Fork has no `/pulse` entry for **60–89 days**, a warning is sent to the lead in `#leads-council`.
  * At **90+ days** of inactivity, an alert goes to staff in `#team-forks` to execute an automated archival of the chapter.
* **Obligations upon Suspension/Revocation:** If a Fork's recognition is archived, suspended, or revoked, the Fork must:
  1. Immediately stop using the bits&bytes™ name, logo, domain, and social media handles.
  2. Hand over all records, digital credentials, assets, and funds to the Foundation.
  3. Cease presenting itself as affiliated with bits&bytes™.
  4. Make no claims to local succession, continuity, or identity ownership.

## 5. Safeguarding, Minor Protection, & IP

### 5.1 Safeguarding Standards
* Safeguarding rules override local autonomy. The safety of minors is the highest priority.
* Mandatory safeguarding checks must be performed by the COO/CCO prior to onboarding any volunteer, contractor, or Fork Lead who will interact with minors.
* **No Unsupervised Adult-Minor Contact:** Adults are prohibited from having direct, unsupervised online or offline contact with minors within Foundation programs.

### 5.2 Minor Intellectual Property (IP)
* Under the **Indian Contract Act, 1872**, minors cannot enter into legally binding contracts. Consequently, direct IP assignments executed by minors are void.
* For minor contributors, IP assignment must be:
  * Signed and executed by a parent or legal guardian on behalf of the minor, OR
  * Deferred until the contributor reaches the age of majority (18 years).
* A Board-approved contributor agreement template must be used.

### 5.3 Escalation Triggers
Pause activity and escalate immediately to `hello@gobitsnbytes.org` if you observe:
* Any safeguarding, bullying, or harassment concerns.
* Funds being moved, collected, or held outside Foundation channels.
* Unauthorised third-party requests for signatures, invoices, or legal guarantees.
* Unauthorised use of the brand name, logo, or assets.

### 6.2 Meeting Scheduler & AI Transcriber ("Meet" Module)
1. **Email Mapping:** Users must register an email via `/meet-email` to receive invitations and `.ics` files.
2. **Scheduling Engine:** Run `/meet-schedule` to schedule (or launch instantly). Routes to `cal.gobitsnbytes.org`. Supports multi-host scheduling and availability management.
3. **Automated VC Provisioning:** For Discord location-type, the bot provisions a temporary VC in the **EVENTS** category 5 minutes prior to the meeting. It pings invitees and starts recording.
4. **Resilience & Debounce:** Debounces empty voice channels for 2 minutes before cleanup. Reconnects recording bots if users join, maintaining a 2-human minimum.
5. **Recording Consent:** Plays audio consent notices in English and Hindi when users join. Posts interactive text warnings in the VC chat with translation options.
6. **Gemini Processing:** Upon meeting completion, processes audio via Gemini to extract meeting summaries, decisions, and action items.
7. **Action-Item Tracker:** Automatically extracts tasks and deadlines. A multi-layer fuzzy matcher resolves speaker names to Discord Snowflake IDs, sending DM notifications with interactive "Mark Completed" and "Dismiss" buttons connected to a local SQLite/Turso database.
8. **Rescheduling:** Host or guest can reschedule up to 3 times via the landing page. Triggers real-time Discord DMs, SMTP email alerts, and Web Push notifications.
